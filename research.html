Reviews on the other Project

The article discusses how Pfizer is using digital wearable devices to capture data on the severity and frequency of itching in patients with atopic dermatitis (AD). The wearable devices use accelerometers and gyroscopes to measure the intensity and frequency of scratching, as well as the location of the itching. The data collected from the devices is then used to improve clinical trials and develop better treatments for AD. The article emphasizes the potential of digital health technologies to improve patient outcomes and provide more precise and personalized care for people with chronic skin conditions. [1]

The article discusses Sanofi's collaboration with a digital health company, Luminostics, to develop a wearable device that can detect night-time scratching in children with atopic dermatitis (AD) without disturbing their sleep. The device uses a microfluidic chip and a smartphone app to detect the presence of certain biomarkers in the child's sweat, which are associated with itching. The goal is to capture more accurate data on the frequency and severity of itching during sleep, which can help improve the management of AD in children. The article highlights the potential of wearable and digital health technologies to provide more personalized care and better outcomes for patients with chronic skin conditions.​ [2]

The article discusses a study by Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis that uses motion detection and machine learning to monitor and analyse scratching behaviours in patients with chronic itching. The researchers developed a wearable device that uses accelerometers and gyroscopes to measure the intensity and frequency of scratching. The data collected from the device is then analysed using machine learning algorithms to identify patterns in scratching behaviour and predict when patients are most likely to experience intense itching. The goal of the study is to improve the understanding and treatment of chronic itching, which can be a debilitating condition for many patients. The article highlights the potential of wearable technology and machine learning to provide more personalized and effective care for patients with chronic conditions. [3]

Itch mechanism

According to a literature review published in the Journal of Dermatological Science, itch is a common and often debilitating symptom that can have a significant impact on quality of life. Itch can be caused by a wide range of conditions, including skin diseases, nerve disorders, and certain medications, and can be difficult to manage due to a lack of effective treatments.

One potential approach to managing itch is the use of machine learning algorithms to identify and classify different types of itch. A number of studies have explored the use of machine learning for this purpose, with some success. For example, a study published in the Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology used machine learning to classify different types of itch based on features such as location, intensity, and duration. Another study, published in the Journal of Dermatological Science, used machine learning to predict the likelihood of itch in patients with atopic dermatitis.

While these studies suggest that machine learning may have some potential for the management of itch, more research is needed to fully understand the capabilities and limitations of this approach. Further studies should aim to validate and expand upon the findings of previous research, as well as explore the use of machine learning in the management of specific types of itch.

In conclusion

The literature suggests that machine learning may be a promising approach for the management of itch, but more research is needed to fully understand its potential and limitations. [4]

Tech Review (Motion Magnification)​

The paper "Learning-based Video Motion Magnification" by Tae-Hyun Oh [5], et al., published at the European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV) in 2018, presents a novel approach for magnifying subtle motion patterns in videos using deep learning techniques.​

The authors propose a method that learns to amplify motion signals in video frames by using a convolutional neural network (CNN) to generate a motion field, which is then applied to the original video frames to produce a motion-magnified output. The proposed method is called "MotionNet," and it is trained on a large dataset of videos with ground truth motion information.​

The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work in video motion analysis and motion magnification, including previous approaches that use optical flow estimation and phase-based methods. The authors also discuss the limitations of these previous methods and highlight the advantages of their proposed approach.​

The experimental results demonstrate that MotionNet can effectively magnify subtle motion patterns in videos, such as the movement of facial features and the pulse of a human wrist. The authors evaluate the performance of their method on various datasets and compare it to other state-of-the-art techniques. The results show that MotionNet outperforms previous methods in terms of visual quality and computational efficiency.​

In conclusion

The paper presents a novel method for learning-based video motion magnification that uses deep learning techniques to amplify subtle motion patterns in videos. The proposed approach outperforms previous methods in terms of visual quality and computational efficiency and has the potential to be applied in various domains, such as healthcare and entertainment.

Review on respiration detection​

The paper "Deep Learning for Healthcare: Review, Opportunities and Challenges" by Daphne Koller and Niraj R. Chokshi, published in the ACM Digital Library in 2018 [6], provides a literature review of the application of deep learning in healthcare, including respiration detection.​

The authors discuss the potential of deep learning in the analysis of physiological signals, including respiration signals. They highlight the challenges associated with traditional methods for respiration detection, such as chest straps and nasal cannulas, and the potential advantages of using deep learning algorithms to analyze signals from non-invasive sensors such as wearable devices.

The paper provides a comprehensive review of the existing literature on the application of deep learning in respiration detection, covering a range of topics including the use of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for signal processing, the detection of apnea and hypopnea in sleep disorders, and the use of deep learning for real-time monitoring of respiration in intensive care units.

The authors discuss the advantages and limitations of deep learning in respiration detection, including the ability to analyze large volumes of data and identify patterns that may not be detectable by traditional methods, as well as the need for high-quality data and the potential for bias in training data.

Finally, the authors identify potential areas for future research in the application of deep learning in respiration detection, including the development of more accurate and robust algorithms for real-time monitoring, the integration of multiple physiological signals, and the exploration of new applications such as the early detection of respiratory diseases.

In conclusion

The paper provides a valuable literature review of the application of deep learning in healthcare, including the detection of respiration. The authors highlight the potential of deep learning in the analysis of physiological signals and identify challenges and opportunities for future research in this area. The paper is a valuable resource for researchers and practitioners in the field of healthcare and machine learning.​

Possible devices​​

Accelerometer - An accelerometer is a device that measures acceleration forces acting on an object. It can be used to detect changes in motion or orientation of an object in three-dimensional space. The accelerometer works based on the principles of Newton's laws of motion. It is measured by sensors such as piezoelectric, capacitive, or strain-gauge sensors. The sensors then convert the physical movement into an electrical signal that can be processed by a computer or other electronic device. Collecting data can be used for motion magnification deep learning data.​

Gyroscope - A gyroscope is a device that measures and maintains orientation and angular velocity. It consists of a spinning wheel or rotor, mounted on a set of gimbals or a solid platform, which is free to rotate in any direction. When the device is rotated or tilted, the rotor experiences a force that causes it to precess, which is the phenomenon of the rotor's axis of rotation moving in a circle. Collecting data can be used with accelerometer for motion magnification deep learning data and for motion detection data.​

​Webcam – is a digital camera that is designed to capture images and videos in real-time and transmit them over the Internet. It is usually connected to a computer via USB or another interface and can be used for a variety of purposes such as video conferencing, live streaming, and recording videos. It can be used to record face of the user to calibrate the facial points.

Sensory electrode - A sensory electrode is a type of electrode that is designed to detect and measure biological signals, such as neural activity or muscle activity, in order to provide information about the function of the nervous system. Sensory electrodes are used in a variety of medical applications, including diagnosis, treatment, and research. It can be used to accurately measure electrical neural signal from the body.

In conclusion

We chose webcam as our possible device choice 

Algorithms and programming related choices​​

For our project algorithm we had several choices to make such as programming language, library and the algorithms choices.

For our programming language, we had two choices in mind, python and C++. Both languages are highly use for AI algorithms.

In the case of C++, this is highly used in ML and AI algorithms as C++ is a compiled language that produces highly efficient code and provides direct memory access and management. This helps in optimizing memory usage for large-scale data processing.

Python has a simpler syntax, which allows for faster development and rapid prototyping. Python has many popular libraries for computer vision. These libraries provide range of features and optimizations.

Overall Conclusion​​​

Overall, we have concluded that building a system that directly detect an itch is impossible due to technology and possible safety issue. And other similar projects that is going on or made do not share same targeted client. Most of the projects' targeted clients are people who do not have the physical disabilities. However, our clients are people who have disabilities and worst case, they can be suffering from quadriplegia.

We chose Python to implement our project as it is easy to prototype many ideas and try out different algorithms. Python also has many libraries like dlib, numpy and pillow which helps in the facial recognition algorithms. It reduces a lot of time when implementing our idea. Python also has backend language, Flask. These are the reasons we choose Python over C++.​

Requirements

Project Background & Partner

The need for assistive robotics for people with disabilities has become increasingly apparent, with around 15% of the world's population living with some form of disability. Many individuals struggle with daily tasks and require assistance. The development of assistive robotics has the potential to significantly improve the quality of life for people with disabilities by providing greater independence.

An industrial project partnership with Avanade aims to create a system that can automatically detect when a person with disabilities has an itch on their face and alert them or a caregiver, using a stretch RE1 robot as a platform and machine learning algorithms to develop an accurate and reliable system. Avanade is a global professional services company that provides consulting, technology, and managed services to help organizations unlock the full potential of their digital investments.​

Project Goal​

The project aims to develop an AI system to detect when people with quadriplegia experience an itch on their face. This will be achieved by comparing facial movements during itchy and non-itchy periods to detect the changes. The system will alert the person or caregiver in real-time so appropriate action can be taken to alleviate discomfort and reduce the risk of skin irritation or infection. The project aims to improve the quality of life for people with quadriplegia and other disabilities starting with an itch.​

Requirement Gathering​

Initial prototype design meeting was conducted with the present of the industrial partner who has interviewed our client beforehand. Weekly feedback from the client and industrial partners.​

We had prepared the interview between Henry Evan and the engineer that is going to handle the RE1 assistive robot with Henry.​

Constant meeting with industrial partner to report progress and gather opinions to achieve shared goal.

Interview 1： From Henry Evans (Potential user)

Q1. Tell me about yourself, what is the status of your body condition? ​

I had a stroke-like attack when I was 40 which left me mute and with severe quadriplegia. Since then, I can only turn my head and use one finger with limitation. All day, I am lying down on my bed and with the help of my wife(caretaker) my daily needs are satisfied.

Q2. What got you interested in the assistive robot?

I saw a TV interview with health-care roboticist Charlie Kemp who used robots to serve as extensions or surrogates for body parts. I gave me a hope to do daily needs by myself and now I am eagerly sharing my research and experience via organization.

Q3. What will be the function of the robot you would need most eagerly? ​

If there could be only one function from the robot, I would choose it to have me scratch my face. Now, I need my wife to scratch my face when I feel itchy but when she is not with me, this itchiness is what irritates me the most. That is why I would choose this function as my most needed function.

Interview 2： From Avanade technician (Cooperate partner)

Q1. How would you set up the system for the User?

I would have to go to their home help the robot map out the living space, then calibrate the facial recognition to work best with the user. I would also have to give them accessible controls so that they can manually operate the robot.​

Q2. What kind of interface are you looking for?​

I would like the interface to display everything the AI sees, not only whether it has detected an Itch. It's important that we're able to determine whether the AI works properly without having to wait for an itch occur.​

Q3. Could you tell me about the robot?

It's a light, small and relatively cheap robot. It consists of a base with a vertical stand, on the stand there is a vertical motor and a stretcher with a claw,. Above the stand is a depth sensor camera in facing forward, which gives a clear image of what the robot is looking at while being able to gauge the distance of objects in it's surroundings.​

MoSCoW List

Must have
- Make an algorithm that can analyse facial movement from a user to detect the presence and approximate location of an itch​
- The algorithm must be calibratable to best adapt to different face​
Should have
- The algorithm should still work if the user moves their head after calibration​
- There should be a testing platform with an accessible UI to use the AI with your webcam​
- The test platform should allow for calibration and configure settings​​
- The test platform should have the option to show facial landmarks to improve testing​
- A web hostable web API to utilise the itch detection algorithm​​
Could have
- The algorithm could be working even with head rotation​​
- Improvement in light sensitivity issue of face recognition​
- The test platform should have the option to show facial landmarks to improve testing​​
Wish have
- The algorithm could add subscribers/listeners that have various functionalities when an itch is detected:​

A robot could assist with the itch when an itch is detected​

The itch data could be sent to a logging database when an itch is detected​

Requirements

Project Background​

The need for assistive robotics for people with disabilities has become increasingly apparent, with around 15% of the world's population living with some form of disability. Many individuals struggle with daily tasks and require assistance. The development of assistive robotics has the potential to significantly improve the quality of life for people with disabilities by providing greater independence. An industrial project partnership with Avanade aims to create a system that can automatically detect when a person with disabilities has an itch on their face and alert them or a caregiver, using a stretch RE1 robot as a platform and machine learning algorithms to develop an accurate and reliable system. Avanade is a global professional services company that provides consulting, technology, and managed services to help organizations unlock the full potential of their digital investments.

Project Goal​​

The project aims to develop an AI system to detect when people with quadriplegia experience an itch on their face. This will be achieved by comparing facial movements during itchy and non-itchy periods to detect the changes. The system will alert the person or caregiver in real-time so appropriate action can be taken to alleviate discomfort and reduce the risk of skin irritation or infection. The project aims to improve the quality of life for people with quadriplegia and other disabilities starting with an itch.​

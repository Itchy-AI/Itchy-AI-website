Project background and partner introduction

The need for assistive robotics for people with disabilities has become increasingly apparent in recent years. According to the World Health Organization, around 15% of the world's population lives with some form of disability, and many of these individuals struggle with daily tasks that many of us take for granted.

People like Henry Evans, who have severe physical disabilities, often require the assistance of caregivers or technology to complete even basic tasks such as scratching an itch. This can be both inconvenient and uncomfortable for the individual, as well as a burden on caregivers who may not always be available to provide assistance.

The development of assistive robotics has the potential to significantly improve the quality of life for people with disabilities by providing them with greater independence and the ability to complete tasks on their own. In this project, we aim to take the first step towards this goal by creating a system that can automatically detect when a person has an itch on their face and alert them or a caregiver so that the itch can be addressed.

We will be using a stretch RE1 robot as a platform for this project, but the focus of the project is on the development of the AI system that will enable the robot to identify and respond to itches. By using machine learning algorithms and data collected from people with disabilities, we hope to create a system that is both accurate and reliable. We believe that this project has the potential to make a meaningful impact on the lives of people with disabilities and pave the way for further development of assistive robotics technology.
